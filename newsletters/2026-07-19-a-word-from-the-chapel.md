# A Word From The Chapel

Beloved friends of the Shepherd's Chapel,

In a world that often demands constant motion and noise, there is a profound grace in simply being still. Gathering in reverence is the first pillar of our worship because it is the quiet soil from which everything else grows. When we step into our unchanging sanctuary, we lay down the week's burdens. It is here, resting in His presence, that we recharge our spirits and find the true strength needed to be His hands and feet in the world.

We witnessed this beautiful expression of reverence just yesterday during our "Painting for Jesus" gathering. Taking the time to create is such a deeply personal form of worship, and the heavenly oil paintings that emerged from our commune were a true testament to the Creator's guiding hand in our lives. I want to extend my heartfelt thanks to everyone who attended and shared their gifts. If you missed it, please ask about when we will host our next session. Whether you are a professional with a brush or entirely new to the canvas, there is always a seat and a warm welcome waiting for you.

![Unsere Kapelle im Sommer](Rev_Marius_Painting_For_jesus.png)

That same guiding hand has been so evident in our shared faith this week. We want to share a joyous update and a deep thank you to everyone who lifted up Martha Barnson's little white Havanese, Gideon. He has just safely recovered from his hip surgery and is feeling so much better. It is a wonderful, comforting reminder of the power of our congregation's prayers and God's gentle, healing touch over all His creation.

![](images/Gemini_Generated_Image_n73ir1n73ir1n73i~2.jpg)

As we look to the days ahead, I invite you to join us in the sanctuary to ground yourself in timeless truth. We will gather for our Sunday worship at 10:00 AM, and our Wednesday Bible study will meet at 7:00 PM. Come exactly as you are—whether in your Sunday best or everyday wear.

## A Thought To Carry

The quiet of the chapel is not empty; it is filled with the steadfast presence of the Lord, waiting to meet you exactly where you are.

Yours in fellowship,
**Reverend Marius**
